    P and L Visit NYC

    4 minute read

    October 8, 2021 - October 10, 2021

    On Friday night, P and L came over to our apartment to stay the weekend. P and L are doctors in residency, and vacation days are hard to come by, so it was nice to see them after such a long time. That night, E presented P with her belated birthday gift - a baby pink robe. P loved it so much that she insisted on keeping both sizes small (for herself) and medium (for L), even though a large would have suited L much better.

    The next morning, E, P, and L (K was unfortunately on tapeout) met up with D and A for brunch at The Osprey in Dumbo. The restaurant was located inside a fancy hotel, and E thought the decor was fantastic. E ordered the Osprey Breakfast, which was surprisingly filling. E even gave her leftovers to L, who was still hungry after finishing his meal. D and A also brought their new puppy C, an adorable one-month old toy maltese, who obediently sat on P’s lap for most of the time.

    Afterwards, D and A drove E, P, and L to their next appointment at the Museum of Ice Cream. Despite being a little late to the event, we were able to sufficiently stuff ourselves with ice cream and various desserts while being bombarded with pink walls and neon lights.

    L was particularly excited to see a basketball hoop, and proceeded to have a dodgeball fight with P, while E took pictures of them the whole time.

    After we finished exploring the museum, E took P and L to Bao Tea House (because we obviously weren’t full enough), and ordered some pork belly, fried chicken, and beef bao, along with a rose grapefruit tea for E. Even though we did not order too much, we all ended up incredibly full, which was not a great sign since P and L had dinner plans with another friend immediately after.

    We then parted ways for the time being, and E headed over to Nyonya to have dinner with K, Steven, and Evelyn, who were staying in the city for a few weeks as well. We ordered the classic half Hainanese chicken (E’s favorite), roti canai, chow kueh teow, and chendol for dessert. Afterwards, E and K felt a bit tired and headed home, while P and L went to a comedy club in the city. When P and L got back, we all relaxed to a few episodes of The Dragon Prince before heading off to bed.

    On Sunday morning, E, P, and L took the train to Chelsea Market, where E excitedly showed off her favorite places to eat. We started off with Los Tacos No. 1, where we ordered the adobada and pollo asado tacos. We all easily agreed that the adobada was probably the best tasting tacos we have ever had, despite P and L living in LA.

    Afterwards, we stopped by Filaga Pizzeria, where we ordered a slice of the Bufalina and Mediterannea. P especially enjoyed the Bufalina, and was impressed by how fresh the mozzarella was. Meanwhile E and L’s favorite was the Mediterannea because of the unique toppings and overall blend of flavors.

    We then stopped by Mokbar to order japchae dumplings and ho cake. P was pleasantly surprised with how tasty the japchae dumplings were, despite them being vegan. At our last stop, we went to The Lobster Place to order the lobster roll and clam chowder. P and L have not had too many lobster rolls in the past, so it was difficult for them to rank, but there was no doubt that the texture and flavor were superb.

    To walk off some of the food we ate, we then did a little shopping in the area. We briefly stopped by Anthropologie just to gawk at the price tags, though there was an interesting display of a small forest made entirely from denim fabric.

    We also stopped by a fragrance store called Sabon, where E bought a little bottle of perfume called White Tea. Finally, we did a little shopping at E and P’s one true love, Aritzia. E bought a sweater, while P tried on a few items and L went upstairs to take a nap on the couch. After our short excursion, it was time to eat again, and we were off to Chinatown to meet up with K and have dinner at Joe’s Shanghai. Luckily, we arrived slightly before the dinner rush, and we were able to get a table after only about twenty minutes of waiting. We started off ordering two stacks of crab and pork soup dumplings. E warned P that these dumplings were the best soup dumplings she has ever had and significantly tastier than Din Tai Fung’s. It seemed like P agreed, since she nearly requested to order two more stacks of soup dumplings! We decided to settle on one more instead. We also ordered some pork dumplings, green beans, and a chili oil fish dish, all of which were thoroughly delicious.

    After we finished our meal, P pulled a sneaky trick on us, saying that she needed to go to the bathroom, but instead generously paid for our entire meal. All too soon, it was time for P and L to take their train back to Connecticut, where L’s parents resided. E and K walked them to their train station where we said our goodbyes, all the while being reminded that we have some pretty amazing friends.
