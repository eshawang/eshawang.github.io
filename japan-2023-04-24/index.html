    Japan 2023 - In Which Two Old Bodies Visit Three Cat Cafes and Walk Fourteen Miles in One Day

    28 minute read

    April 24, 2023 - May 3, 2023

    It was a long flight from Newark to Tokyo, and as comfortable as having a large snoring Samoan man asleep on one’s shoulder could be. Between pushing off the heavy advances and the mid airplane food, which Esha kept squirreling and/or forcing Kelvin to eat, they squeezed in a couple of movies. Don’t Worry Darling had an interesting premise but felt a bit lacking in its conclusion, while Catch Me If You Can was a more captivating watch (and long overdue).

    When they finally landed at Narita, Esha and Kelvin made their way through some dubiously working customs machines to be welcomed by a large number of Nintendo ads.

    Kelvin exchanged their vouchers for the JR Pass, and they took the NRT Express to Tokyo Station. The station was ridiculously large and difficult to find their way out of, but they eventually got above ground and headed towards their hotel in Ginza.

    Kelvin called for an obligatory stop at the first Lawson they saw, and Esha quickly fell in love with Japanese convenience stores as they loaded up on onigiri, ready-to-eat veggies(!!), and fruity drinks. After checking in and getting bombarded by Kelvin’s nuclear socks, they took a much needed shower and nap, greatly enjoying the comfy hotel pajamas.

    When they woke at 11 pm, they tentatively headed to Gyoza Hohei, walking through the shopping district and finding a Dior store without even trying. At the restaurant, what Esha and Kelvin thought would be a 20 minute wait for a seat was actually the wait for food, and they excitedly wolfed down a chicken ginger soup and super thin and light gyoza with garlic, chives, and ginger fillings.

    They stopped by Pompadour Bakery on the walk back for some delicious tomato-filled fried pastries and were surprised by the number of people and shops open at 1 am (and, of course, how clean the streets were)!

    Esha dragged Kelvin to both Family Mart and 7-11 so she could compare and contrast the store offerings. Finally, they attempted to go back to sleep as Kelvin belatedly learned some Japanese phrases from Youtube in bed.

    The two of them woke up super early in the morning, so they walked to Tsukiji fish market to watch the stores open and get ahead of the crowds. As they browsed the food stalIs, they munched on a fish cake skewer with shrimp and scallop, tamago chunks, and egg milk drink.

    Esha and Kelvin settled on breakfast at Kouragumi Tsukiji as it opened, ordering a delectable sea urchin and salmon roe bowl, grilled eel, abalone, and scallop with even more sea urchin. They enjoyed the fresh seafood with copious amounts of free green tea, and by the time they were finished eating, the restaurant was completely full with a line waiting for their seat.

    After their meal, they walked through the rest of Tsukiji to Namiyoke Shrine, home to two stone dragon heads, and were enticed by inari and wagyu skewers, though they decided against more food for the moment.

    Next, they visited Hamarikyu Gardens for some beautiful views of the seawall and southern Tokyo. Esha and Kelvin did a small squats workout by the water, and as they continued over the Umiteozutai bridge, they were surprised to see blue jellyfish of different sizes floating in the pond! As they took photos and videos, an epically huge school of fish swam by as well.

    Past a couple more bridges and some old falconry bells, they arrived at Nakajima-no-ochaya, where they indulged Kelvin’s old man hobby of relaxing by the water with matcha and white bean cake.

    Unfortunately they couldn’t rest for long, since 10 am rolled around, and the two of them had to hustle over for Dior’s opening. Esha was a woman on a mission, taking advantage of the Yen-USD exchange rate to purchase a very discounted Lady Dior while simultaneously giving her SA the most efficient commission/time ever. As they left the store to head to lunch, they were caught off guard by the employees’ 3-pronged 90° deep bow.

    Over to Shinjuku, they met up with Jenny and David at the FMA pop-up cafe at Paselabo Tower. Kelvin was deeply disappointed with Esha’s anime memory as they perused the novelty shop, and he had to reintroduce her to Roy Mustang’s cardboard cutout. When they got their table, they all ordered Edward-themed drinks and opened collector coasters - Kelvin got the Elric brothers, while Esha got Chinese people she also couldn’t remember. Everyone enjoyed some Armstrong curry and Winry apple pie, and then David went back to work when they couldn’t decide on second lunch.

    Jenny joined Esha and Kelvin for the afternoon, starting with a walk to Tokyo Metropolitan Government building, on which Kelvin annoyed her with his gaijin drifting to the right side of the street. They took the elevator to the 45th floor observation deck for some great views of the city, though Mt. Fuji was of course hidden behind the clouds.

    Second lunch was finally agreed upon, and the three of them made their way to Ebisu Ramen Kamuro for paitan, which was incredibly delicious and Esha’s first experience at a ticket vending machine restaurant.

    After stuffing themselves, they rode the subway to Harajuku, and Kelvin further embarrassed the local by paying cash for tickets and dropping coins all over the floor. They did a short hike to Meiji Jingu, where he correctly prayed to the shrine despite Jenny’s misinformation on bowing procedure, and when it started to drizzle, the girls took the chance to do some makeup shopping at the nearby department stores.

    When Kelvin began to doze off, Esha had the best idea of the day - cat cafe! David rejoined the three of them at nearby Mocha Cat Cafe, and they enjoyed unlimited drinks, manga to read, and SO MANY KITTIES. The cafe had great decor, especially the central cat tree and hanging platforms, and even the bathroom wall was lined with portraits of the cafe cats. Kelvin pet Firefly the orange kitty for a long time, and Esha reprimanded him for being annoying and poking the toe beans while Firefly’s head was in a lamp.

    Eventually, they all said goodbye to the cats and made their way back to Ginza Six to eat a late dinner at Nakamura Tokichi. They had delicious matcha parfaits with so many fun layers and matcha soba with tofu, whose super bouncy noodles and seasoned tofu were an instant favorite for Esha. Way too stuffed, they decided against Jenny’s favorite pastime of salaryman-watching, but bought some premium matcha and took a look at the bookstore upstairs until KeIvin had to crash. Esha and he were pretty proud of staying awake until 9, thinking they had done well to fight off the jet lag…then they woke up together at 1 am gg.

    They started the (very early) morning with a study of cat genitalia, followed by a couple hours of existential crisis. Kelvin was in the shower when they received a text from their new cat sitter, who explained to them that Blossom was in fact a boy, and he had to sit down in the bathtub to collect himself. Though the news was shocking, Esha and Kelvin realized pretty quickly that Blossom’s size and behavior now made a lot of sense, and he had probably been trying to tell them his true gender identity for a while.

    When they were finally able to get up to go to breakfast, it was raining out, but they were able to borrow a pair of clear umbrellas from the hotel. They felt very anime as they walked and chatted and repeatedly messed up their cats’ pronouns. Esha allowed Kelvin to fulfill his dream of 7 years of eating at Yoshinoya in Japan, which turned out to be much yummier than in the US. Though they embarrassed themselves with ordering off the extensive Japanese-only menu, exploding a salad dressing packet, and getting lapped by the customers seated next to them, they very much enjoyed the beef curry and unagi dons.

    Their next stop was the Muji flagship store, where Esha was like a kid on Christmas morning as they browsed floor-to-ceiling shelves of snacks, soup bases, and breads. Making their way up 6(!) floors, Kelvin and she went on a bit of a shopping spree, buying pajama sets after enjoying the comfy ones from the hotel, chopsticks because why not, and a check-in suitcase for the inevitable snack haul.

    Outside, it became really humid from the summer rain, so they dropped off their purchases at the hotel, then went to Tokyo Station to make seat reservations for their upcoming Shinkansen trips. The ticket agents were very helpful, and the process easier than expected, and with that out of the way, they headed to Akihabara to see Origami Kaikan. They were wowed by the samurai-themed models and got a glimpse of the paper-making studio, then Esha had fun feeling all the different types of origami paper in the gift shop.

    With a couple rolls of pretty patterns in hand, they walked on to another Paselabo Tower, passing by a Miyazaki-like shrine and torii in the middle of a city street.

    Unfortunately, they hadn’t had the foresight to reserve a seat at Eorzea Cafe beforehand, and they arrived to find it completely full and booked out for weeks. Kelvin had to settle for a paladin pose photo in front of the FFXIV posters, then Esha, feeling hungry, pulled out Jenny’s recommendation list and led them to Tsukiji Gindaco for a unique croissant taiyaki with custard filling and deliciously seasoned takoyaki.

    To kill time before real dinner, they browsed through the many floors of nearby Akiba department store and came away with two jackets from Uniqlo and Kelvin’s first pair of Adidas Sam Smiths to replace his stupid bubbling canvas shoes. The hunt for a good hair clip continued to bear no fruit at Daiso, though there was more pretty origami and cute home decor. Kelvin marveled at the feeling of dry socks as they walked to 炭火焼鳥 駿 yakitori to meet up again with Jenny and David, this time joined as well by Megan and Harris. It was nice catching up on their lives abroad and sharing FFXIV glams while devouring every imaginable chicken organ.

    They saw Mocha over doggy cam and brainstormed boy names for Blossom, then after dinner they all went to 21 Ni Ice for some novel cream drinks. Esha enjoyed the mango cream flavor with coconut jelly, and Kelvin especially liked the corn flakes at the bottom. After some obligatory insta selfies inside the cramped and crowded store, and a quick pass of a nearby Don Quijote, they headed back to their hotel to prepare for an early morning trip to Nikko State Park the next day.

    Esha and Kelvin rose at 6 am and hunted for breakfast before their Shinkansen ride to Utsunomiya, settling on salmon and tuna mayo onigiri and a chicken ekiben from Tokyo station. The meal made Kelvin very happy for cheap and healthy fast food, though it was somewhat offset by the Muji snacks they brought along.

    As the two of them settled into their seats, they munched on garlic sauce potato chips, matcha chocolate strawberries, and fruit jellies. It was a nice clear day after the rain, and they got to see Mt. Fuji in the backdrop beyond the Tokyo outskirts!

    Upon reaching Utsunomiya, they transferred to the JR Nikko line for another hour long train ride before arriving in town. KeIvin gathered some info at the tourism station and ultimately decided against visiting Lake Chuzenji this time after hearing Jenny’s horror stories of getting stuck on the one way roads. Esha and he walked through the quaint little town districts, scoping out various dessert and snack shops on the way to the World Heritage site and the famous Shinkyo Bridge.

    They first hiked up to Shirato Falls and Takino’o Shrine, getting a nice workout and breathing in the fresh mountain air.

    After coming back down, they went to the highly recommended Nikko Coffee for lunch, which was probably their favorite meal of the trip up to that point. Along with Nikko blend coffee and muscat tea, the cafe served unique variations of Japanese comfort foods. The delicious omu-fried-rice with rich tomato sauce really hit the spot for Kelvin, while Esha delighted in the fall-off-the-bone soft pork in slightly sweet black curry. For dessert, a chocolate gateau cake with hints of miso with ice cream that had a sorbet feel while still being creamy left them so full. They purchased some strawberry milk rice crackers and gold foil kitty bread for Jenny and David from nearby stores before returning to their sightseeing.

    With combo tickets, they saw the Rinnoji temple and treasure house, which had a beautiful shoyoen behind it, and Taiyu-in temple hall, where Iemitsu Tokugawa lied. They also passed by the Gohunoto five-story pagoda and Toshugu shrine. After finishing their round of the World Heritage site, Esha and Kelvin started to hike up to Jakko Falls, but they turned back due to lack of shade and trail. Instead, they switched targets to Kanmangafuchi abyss, which was guarded by little statues which the locals had dressed in cute hats and scarves for warmth. The gorge itself was made up of interesting rock formations that looked like they were carved out by cats.

    Pretty tired from the day’s activities, they slowly walked back to town, stopping at Nikko Pudding Tei for creme brulee and sakura puddings. Esha made flapping crane origami from 15 year old muscle memory and gave one to the store employees.

    She and Kelvin enjoyed a long nappy ride back to Utsunomiya and Tokyo; they changed lines to Shinjuku at Omiya to get dinner at Tempura Shinjuku Tsunahachi, where Jenny and David graciously waited in line for seats for them. Kelvin ordered the basic tempura zen with regular shrimp, and Esha upgraded hers with fresh Japanese tiger prawns, which were shown and killed in front of them - a shocking experience! Esha’s guilt was assuaged by their deliciousness, as the various vegetable and seafood tempura were made hot with perfect amounts of batter.

    Afterwards, Jenny led them through Ryu no Miyako Inshokugai, a bar with multiple floors of different themes, which was a total vibe. They traded food gifts before parting ways, and Kelvin unabashedly ran onto the train back to Ginza to grab a seat for Esha, whose ankle hurt after the long day of rocky hiking.

    The next morning, they continued onwards to Kyoto - on the bullet train, they snacked on the amazing I’m Donut? with matcha filling and assorted snacks (those were indeed words). When a trolley came by, Esha was reminded of the Hogwarts Express and excitedly bought an onigiri bento, which had plum and pickled veggies, for only 480Y.

    After just a few hours, they arrived, dropped off their bags, and took the JR train out to Arashiyama. They were immediately faced with so much street food and started going to town, buying a chocolate ice cream banana crepe (rip truffle), matcha warabimochi (so soft and chewy!), and karaage skewer.

    The two of them passed by even more shops as they crossed Togetsukyo Bridge over the Katsura River to hike up to the monkey park atop Iwatayama Peak. They arrived just in time for the macaques’ scheduled feeding and witnessed a delightful frenzy of antics, including a few screaming disputes.

    As things calmed down, Esha and Kelvin sat by the pool, wishing they could jump in with the monkeys, then entered the cage for humans to watch others hand out peanuts and apple slices.

    Back outside, they took banana pictures and admired the view of Kyoto before doting on a mama with baby macaque that had food coma’ed in the middle of nursing.

    After hiking back down the mountain, they grabbed a matcha milk soft serve with rice cracker ducks, which somehow disappeared when Esha wasn’t looking.

    To add insult to injury, they discovered they had just missed the Zen Buddhist vegetarian lunch when they arrived at Tenryu-ji. The stroll through the temple and garden was still nice, and the latter led them to the Arashiyama bamboo grove. They passed by many women in kimonos along the photogenic forest path, and Kelvin made up the missed noms to Esha with more gyoza and a crab stick at the end.

    On the ride to Emmachi station, they stood behind the conductor in the first car and got a cool view of the tracks.

    Kinkaku-ji was a 45 minute walk from there, and Esha and Kelvin began to feel their age, as their leg and ankle started to hurt from the 20+ miles they had done in the last two days. They made it through a slight bit of drizzle and took some op pictures of the golden pavilion, then, after finishing the temple tour, they visited a nearby Mangetsu store for red bean filled mochi and jelly.

    Kelvin found a great looking udon shop, Ichibaku Nanasai, not too far away, which had a bowl with seasonal veggies and kitsune that was so fresh and delicious, and another one with an uni cream sauce packed with flavor. The noodles were so good that Kelvin had to order a third bowl with suji curry, while Esha watched very impressed. Apparently, Kelvin impressed the restaurant staff too, as the maitre d’ commented excitedly while he was in the bathroom, and Esha smiled and patted her belly. With a thank you and “oishii”, the two of them took the bus back to the hotel, and, utterly exhausted, Kelvin snored through the night to Esha’s detriment.

    Esha wanted more mochi the next day (even though they hadn’t finished their Mangetsu ones yet), so they visited the shopping mall under Kyoto Station after they managed to roll out of bed. They bought some yummy shrimp and cream corn croquettes and ate while they browsed the sweets section.

    Glad that they had stopped by a Mangetsu the day before when they saw the line in the station wrap around three times, they diversified with another shop that was selling fruit-filled mochi. Though the chestnut flavor was sadly sold out, the combo special blew Esha’s mind.

    With a promise to come back for more later, the two of them continued on their way to the Gion district and walked along Kamo River. They were drawn into a matcha store, where they bought tea tins for family, and then they briefly toured Maruyama Park, Yasaka-jinja Shrine, and Chion-in Temple.

    For lunch, they had a reservation at Rengetsu-jaya, a ten-course tofu kaiseki recommended to them by Jenny, which was super creative in the different ways it prepared and presented the many gluten dishes. The agar was especially novel, and the stringy cheesy tofu gratin was fun to eat. Oolong tea and a sweet plum liquor that Kelvin actually liked accompanied the meal, and Esha again marveled at Kelvin’s appetite as she gave him her rice and pickles and wondered if he was miserable/hungry all the time (yes). Dessert was another red bean filled mochi wrapped in shiso leaf that Esha could not finish and Kelvin had no problem devouring.

    Super impressed with the meal, they walked off their lunch to Yoshidayama, passing by the enormous Heian-jingu Grand Torii in front of Kyocera Kyoto Museum of Art.

    After a semi-lengthy hike through some hilly residential areas, they managed to find Mo-an Tea House and snag two seats on the back couch without a reservation. Google Lens saved the day by translating the Japanese-only menu, and they ordered iced yuzu juice and matcha drink sets with a matcha cheesecake. They relaxed a bit, munching on their cookies and snacks while listening to the rain outside, but mosquitoes soon came to munch on them as well. In the end Esha came away with one bite while Kelvin had more than one.

    They made their way down the mountain and visited Ginkaku-ji, Kinkaku-ji’s lower tier sibling, a short walk away. The garden was quite nice and refreshing in the rain, and afterwards the two of them continued down Philosopher’s Path.

    Unfortunately there was no kitty sighting this time around, but they made their own luck and headed over to PuchiMarry cat cafe near Nishiki Market. Kelvin had some massive gaijin struggles as he stepped into the cafe without slippers, wore the slippers back outside to store his umbrella, then had to change slippers going back in. To top it all off, he allowed two cats to run into the reception room as he entered the kitty area. In the end though, Esha and he had a great time feeding cat popsicle to derpy “David” (who turned out to be a girl), meeting feline-corgi hybrid “Hermione”, and fanboying Koromo, the cutest folded-ears orange kibbie. They scritched some cats to sleep and witnessed many sploots and also a cat sex dungeon.

    After feeding time, they headed back to the hotel, walking through Nishiki Market and getting a delicious Torikara stick and briefly considering Kura Revolving Sushi.

    On their last day in Kyoto, Esha and Kelvin woke early to the pitter patter and headed to the train station, timing the rain stopping perfectly with their arrival at Fushimi Inari Shrine. There weren’t too many people yet, so they explored the shrine area a bit before starting the hike up the n-degree bad spirit filter. Esha’s outfit happened to match the bright orange torii very well!

    Partway up the mountain, they met a stray black cat, which was the moment Esha had been waiting for the entire trip. She proudly busted out her bag of Temptation snacks, and their new void friend wolfed down half of it while many strangers stopped and watched.

    After finally cutting him off for fear of his health, Esha and Kelvin continued to a clearing before the big loop trail, where they enjoyed inari on Inari made with delicious sesame seed rice and a soy bean flour soft serve.

    While exploring the rest of the mountain, they came across a number of foxes in scarves and a real tiny frog, as they struggled to get pictures without other tourists in them.

    When the two of them finished their hike, they headed back to Kyoto Station, where Esha got two more of her favorite mochi ever and also finally a satisfactory (but overpriced) hair clip, and then continued on to Nijo Castle. Esha was suddenly hit with a(nother) gyoza craving, and Kelvin found Ebisugawa Gyoza Nakajima, a small hole in the wall restaurant with an amazing lunch set. They devoured the dumplings, chashu, and veggies and learned to mix the optimal gyoza sauce.

    Nearby was a funny shop called Banana Juice for Human by Gorilla, where Kelvin bought a chocolate chip banana milk and got yelled at by gorilla while ordering.

    Over at the castle, they toured the inside and learned about shogun v. emperor politics and symbolism of power shifts through the architecture. They got a nice view of the surrounding moat and gardens from a watchtower, then they walked to Kyoto Gyoen to see the outside of the Imperial Palace. There were some locals walking their big fuzzy doggos.

    The doggos drew Esha and Kelvin’s attention until 5 pm rolled by, and they hit up Kikyo Sushi at opening time. It was a good thing they did, since at 5:22 people started getting turned away without reservations. They went hard, ordering Sakura and Kikyo sushi sets with extra toro and uni nigiri. Kelvin loved the toro and chutoro, and the salmon and amberjack had great mouthfeel. Esha found the snapper a bit stringy but really enjoyed the uni, roe, and rest of the sushi, showing her huge progress in learning to eat raw fish over the last year.

    Very satisfied, they returned to their hotel to rest their feet (and Esha’s old lady hip) before heading back out at night to a special To-Ji Temple light-up. They loved the surreal views and cool reflection shots, as Esha creeped behind some pro photographers to find the best camera angles.

    The trip back crossed the 14(!) mile mark for the day, and the two of them snacked on salt and nori chips, sakura gummies, and Spy x Family Hi-Chew to reward/recuperate their broken bodies before washing up and passing out.

    Esha and Kelvin checked out the next morning, bought the last pack of sakura gummies from Family Mart, and took the JR train to Nara. They stored their bags in a convenient coin locker at the station and headed to the park, passing by lots of street food and shops on the way. Kelvin wanted a melon pan with pistachio ice cream, which was so warm, sweet, and flaky, like a cookie made of bread.

    When they got near Kofuku-ji, it was instantly deer! Kelvin bought two stacks of crackers and taught Esha to bow before feeding, and they laughed watching the deer emote spam back.

    Before long, they found themselves again facing a 3-pronged 90° deep bow attack, but this time there was lasting damage, as one particularly aggro boy chomped on both Kelvin’s pants and Esha’s leg. She left with a red mark and became a strong advocate for deer safety. With the crackers gone, they had a more relaxed time giving pets and scritches to a resting herd. Kelvin’s no-ears didn’t quite work but he enjoyed the cute ear flaps nonetheless.

    For lunch, they sat down at Mizuya Chaya and ordered delicious cold udon with veggies, a side of lightly fried karaage, and ice green tea milk. They ate by the stream and witnessed a deer come looking for food and grab a piece of paper off a nearby couple’s tray. She tried a shrimp tempura too but found it untasty and spit it out.

    In the afternoon, Esha and Kelvin saw some awkward teenage deer with half-shed spotted coats hanging out with their parents near the temples.

    Before leaving Nara, they strolled down a shopping street, picking up some Amarou strawberry soft serve, yuzu juice and Bonbon biscuits, and a premium pack of delightfully roasted gyokuro leaves.

    They got their luggage back from the coin locker and took the Shinkansen to Osaka; as usual, they got lost in the train station for a while before finding their way to E Street and their hotel. There were too many food options in the bustling neighborhood, and in his decision paralysis Kelvin semi-seriously suggested Kura, and Esha agreed to put their names down while they browsed. Somehow they didn’t see the translations for the waitlist screen, and they submitted as a party of two children. As they browsed the rest of E Street, they came upon a stand up shop selling 500Y katsudon, which Kelvin happily ordered. They demolished the deliciously hearty meal well within the Kura wait time.

    When they did get seated at the revolving sushi bar, they had fun picking out a bunch of specials and legit sushi that they wouldn’t have dared order back in the states. The salmon belly and marinated aged tuna were quite enjoyable, as well as the shrimp-avocado-crab Demon Slayer collab. Beef and pork nigiri were solid too, and the corn mayo roll was a sleeper. Kelvin ordered a JP exclusive rice coke drink and made the mistake of putting it back on the conveyor belt to take a picture…luckily no one was sitting at the end table as Esha ran to retrieve it. For all the work and embarrassment, the drink tasted exactly like coke with rice. Not sure what Kelvin expected. Eventually they finished their feast - overall, Kura was quite good compared to the restaurants in the U.S., but it was mid for Japan - only 132Y per plate though!

    Afterwards, they explored the shops at Osaka Station, including the One Piece and Nintendo stores. Esha considered buying lots of Zelda merch but ultimately settled on a super cute Pikmin shirt.

    When the stores closed, they walked over to Umeda Sky Building and took the elevator to the 34th floor, followed by a cool escalator up to the sky deck. The two of them enjoyed the nighttime view of Osaka and the pretty, albeit confusing, animation that played on the TV. They browsed the gift shop for a bit then finally went back to the hotel to hydrate Esha (no rabies pls) and sleep.

    In the morning, they went back to Osaka Station for some Mister Donut, ordering matcha mochi and custard donuts to try. Unfortunately, they did not compare to I’m Donut? and were definitely overhyped online.

    Kelvin also discovered that the Museum of Housing and Living was closed, so Esha and he skipped getting off the train at Temma Station and rode the JR loop around to Osakajokoen. They walked leisurely towards Osaka Castle, passing by Osaka-Jo Hall and a couple of tree groves.

    At the pier, they bought timed tickets for the golden gozabune ride before crossing the bridge and climbing up to the castle park. The line outside the castle was ridiculously long, so Esha and Kelvin gathered around some street performers instead. They watched a very talented yo-yo player for a while, as well as a one-man percussion section with a traditional long horn.

    The rest of the park was quite serene and home to many birds - some were having a girls day out while others were hard at work building nests over the small pond. Esha and Kelvin then took the 20 minute gozabune ride around the inner castle moat. They saw the human face rock disease and learned that northeast was an unlucky direction (RIP NY). There were more birds and some turtles sunbathing by the shore, and Osaka Castle was particularly photogenic from the boat.

    After their tour, they headed to Namba Station and Dotonbori Street, where their first stop was Hanamaruken Namba Hozenji. Esha and Kelvin ordered one of their famous pork rib ramen, one “happy” ramen, and a side of gyoza. Kelvin ended up eating half of Esha’s noodles since they were so delicious, and they happily slurped up all of the savory pork bone broth.

    They continued their walk down Dotonbori Main Street and lined up for Kururu Kuromon Museum upon spotting the signature giant octopus above the storefront. However, unlike Kelvin’s experience seven years ago, this time the takoyaki was super watery and couldn’t even be picked up without falling apart. Quite disappointed, they learned their lesson to check (and believe!) recent reviews before deciding to order.

    The two of them moved on to Shinbaishi and shopped at the big Daiso, where they went back and forth on buying more cute origami paper, which turned out to be only 110Y.

    To complete the trifecta, they visited Neko Tails for 3/3 cat cafes per city. This one was a unique experience with bean bag chairs and blankets for each customer, and the owners came around to place cats in people’s laps. Esha and Kelvin had a relaxing time feeding and petting all the nappy kitties and leafing through the book of lap sit ratings.

    Afterwards, Kelvin made a stop for Cremia Ice Cream, made with super creamy Hokkaido milk, and Esha got an iced coffee with lemon jelly that was surprisingly good as well. The two took a walk along Dotonbori River as the sun set, reflecting on their travels and time in Japan, and loaded up on snacks before heading to the hotel, grabbing their bags, and taking their last Shinkansen back to Tokyo.

    For the final half-day, Esha and Kelvin woke up extra early and still had to book it to TeamLab Planets in Chuo City. Despite getting lost looking for a seemingly non-existent subway entrance, they got there for their time slot and were ushered into the locker room.

    With bare feet and rolled up pants, they entered the first water area, walking upstream through some cool ambience to a waterfall.

    In the next room, they hopped, flopped, and rolled through a bean bag room, which was great fun as they watched others struggling around them.

    Through the exit, they arrived into the insta-famous hanging LEDs display. The sequences of lights cycled through sparkly forest, musical rainbow, and Star Wars warp speed, which were all greatly accentuated by the surrounding mirror geometry.

    After taking many photos between the light threads, and starting to get conscious about the time, they continued on to their favorite room, a koi projection “pond” with very soothing music.

    They lingered for a good while chasing fish before heading through a maze of big balloon balls.

    The last section of the water area was a rotating kaleidoscope dome of flowers, which made for some epic videos (but trippy photos).

    The garden half of TeamLabs was a lot shorter and included a silver eggs display and hanging flower garden.

    Esha and Kelvin crawled through the lively vines to arrive back at the lockers, where they retrieved their shoes then rushed to meet up with Jenny and David for one last meal near Tokyo Station. They ordered Italian food for a change of pace and grabbed terrace seats on the 7th floor of the Marunouchi building. The sea bream broccolini pasta and large margherita+ pizza were deliciously prepared, and the dough super soft.

    After lunch, they browsed Character Street together and obtained more snacks before Esha and Kelvin had to catch the NRT Express and say their goodbyes.

    At the airport, Kelvin went on a long but unsuccessful mission for Hibiki/Yamazaki whiskey for Joel (RIP bonus). The flight back home was more spacious, and Esha and he watched The Tunnel to Summer, the Exit of Goodbyes and The Whale, which were both uniquely artistic and emotional. They caught some z’s, whined about how the Japanese airline food was notably better, and shared in their excitement to get back to their girl AND BOY kibbies.

    A final summary of Esha and Kelvin’s Japan walk-a-thon:

    And overall cat cafe rating: Kyoto > Tokyo > Osaka.
